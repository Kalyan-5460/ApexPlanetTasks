Common Admission Test (CAT)

Gateway to India's Premier Management Institutes

What is CAT?

The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a computer-based test for admission to graduate management programs in India. Conducted annually by the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) on a rotational basis, CAT is one of the most competitive exams in the country.

CAT Exam Structure

The CAT exam tests candidates on three sections:

Section Description Duration Questions
Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC) Tests reading comprehension, verbal reasoning, and grammar 40 minutes 24
Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DILR) Tests ability to interpret data and solve logical problems 40 minutes 20
Quantitative Ability (QA) Tests mathematical skills and numerical ability 40 minutes 22

Note: The exam duration is 120 minutes (2 hours) with 40 minutes allocated to each section. There's a negative marking of -1 for each wrong answer in MCQs, but no negative marking for non-MCQ questions.

Eligibility Criteria

To appear for CAT, candidates must fulfill the following criteria:

  • Hold a Bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks or equivalent CGPA (45% for SC, ST, and PWD candidates)
  • The degree must be from a recognized university or educational institution
  • Final year students can also apply (their admission will be provisional)
  • There is no age limit to appear for CAT

CAT Preparation Tips

Preparing for CAT requires a strategic approach:

1. Understand the Exam Pattern

Familiarize yourself with the exam structure, question types, and marking scheme.

2. Create a Study Plan

Divide your time effectively among all three sections based on your strengths and weaknesses.

3. Focus on Basics

Strengthen your fundamentals in quantitative aptitude, grammar, and logical reasoning.

4. Practice Mock Tests

Regularly take full-length mock tests to improve time management and identify weak areas.

5. Work on Speed and Accuracy

Develop strategies to solve questions quickly without compromising accuracy.

6. Read Regularly

Improve reading speed and comprehension by reading newspapers, magazines, and quality literature.

Top B-Schools Accepting CAT Scores

While IIMs are the primary institutions that accept CAT scores, several other top B-schools also consider CAT performance:

  • All Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs)
  • Faculty of Management Studies (FMS), Delhi
  • SPJIMR, Mumbai
  • Management Development Institute (MDI), Gurgaon
  • Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT)
  • National Institute of Industrial Engineering (NITIE), Mumbai
  • Institute of Management Technology (IMT), Ghaziabad
  • TA Pai Management Institute (TAPMI), Manipal
