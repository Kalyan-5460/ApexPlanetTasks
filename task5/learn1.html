Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE)

Your Gateway to Higher Education and PSU Opportunities

What is GATE?

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is a national-level examination conducted jointly by the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) and seven Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) on behalf of the National Coordination Board (NCB)-GATE, Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Education (MoE), Government of India.

GATE is a gateway to higher education and career opportunities

Importance of GATE

GATE serves multiple purposes:

  • Admission to Postgraduate Programs: GATE score is used for admission to Master's programs and direct Doctoral programs in Engineering/Technology/Architecture, and Doctoral programs in relevant branches of Science, in Institutions supported by the MoE and other Government agencies.
  • Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs): Many PSUs like BHEL, GAIL, HPCL, IOCL, NTPC, etc. use GATE scores for recruitment.
  • Scholarships: Qualified candidates may become eligible for financial assistance in M.Tech/M.E programs.
  • International Opportunities: Some universities abroad consider GATE scores for admission.

GATE Exam Pattern

The GATE exam is conducted online as a Computer Based Test (CBT). The exam duration is 3 hours, and it consists of 65 questions worth 100 marks.

Section Number of Questions Marks
General Aptitude 10 15
Engineering Mathematics 5-10 13-15
Subject Questions 45-50 70-72

Note: There are negative marks for wrong answers in MCQs. For 1-mark MCQs, 1/3 mark is deducted, and for 2-mark MCQs, 2/3 mark is deducted. There is no negative marking for Numerical Answer Type (NAT) questions.

Preparation Tips

Effective preparation is key to success in GATE:

Effective preparation strategy for GATE

  1. Understand the Syllabus: Thoroughly go through the official syllabus of your subject.
  2. Create a Study Plan: Allocate time for each subject based on its weightage and your proficiency.
  3. Standard Reference Books: Use recommended books and study materials.
  4. Practice Previous Year Papers: Solve at least 5-10 years' question papers.
  5. Take Mock Tests: Regular mock tests help in time management and identifying weak areas.
  6. Focus on Concepts: GATE tests conceptual understanding rather than rote learning.
  7. Revision: Regular revision is crucial to retain concepts.
