Study Abroad Exams: GRE, TOEFL & IELTS

Your comprehensive guide to standardized tests for international education

1. GRE (Graduate Record Examinations)

The GRE is a standardized test that is an admissions requirement for many graduate schools in the United States, Canada, and other English-speaking countries.

Key Features:

  • Measures verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, analytical writing, and critical thinking skills
  • Computer-delivered test available year-round
  • Scores valid for 5 years
  • Test duration: About 3 hours 45 minutes
  • Score range: 130-170 for Verbal and Quantitative, 0-6 for Analytical Writing

The GRE General Test is accepted by thousands of graduate and business schools worldwide. Some programs may also require GRE Subject Tests in specific disciplines.

2. TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)

The TOEFL test measures your ability to use and understand English at the university level and evaluates how well you combine your listening, reading, speaking, and writing skills.

Key Features:

  • Accepted by more than 11,000 universities in over 150 countries
  • Internet-based test (iBT) is the most common format
  • Test duration: About 3 hours
  • Score range: 0-120 (combined from four sections scored 0-30 each)
  • Scores valid for 2 years

The TOEFL iBT measures academic English skills the way they're actually used in the classroom, making it the most accepted English-language test in the world.

3. IELTS (International English Language Testing System)

IELTS is the world's most popular English language proficiency test for higher education and global migration, with over 3 million tests taken in the past year.

Key Features:

  • Two versions: Academic (for study) and General Training (for migration)
  • Test duration: 2 hours 45 minutes
  • Band score range: 1-9 for each section (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking)
  • Scores valid for 2 years
  • Accepted by over 10,000 organizations worldwide

IELTS uses a one-on-one speaking test to assess your English communication skills, which some find more realistic than computerized speaking tests.

Preparation Tips for Study Abroad Exams

General Preparation Strategies:

  1. Start early: Begin preparing at least 3-6 months before your test date.
  2. Understand the test format: Familiarize yourself with question types and timing.
  3. Take practice tests: Regular full-length practice tests help build stamina and identify weak areas.
  4. Build vocabulary: Especially important for GRE Verbal and TOEFL/IELTS reading sections.
  5. Improve time management: Learn to pace yourself during the test.
  6. Focus on weak areas: After practice tests, dedicate more time to improving your weakest sections.
  7. Use official materials: ETS (for GRE/TOEFL) and Cambridge/British Council/IDP (for IELTS) provide the most accurate preparation materials.
