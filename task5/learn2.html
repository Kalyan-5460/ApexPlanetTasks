Introduction to UPSC Civil Services Exam

Logo of Union Public Service Commission

The UPSC Civil Services Examination (CSE) is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) to recruit candidates for various civil services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and other central services.

Considered one of the toughest competitive exams in the world, the UPSC CSE tests candidates on a wide range of subjects to select the most capable administrators for the country.

Exam Structure

UPSC Civil Services Exam Pattern

The UPSC Civil Services Examination consists of three successive stages:

  1. Preliminary Examination (Prelims) - Objective type screening test
  2. Main Examination (Mains) - Descriptive written examination
  3. Personality Test (Interview) - Final selection round

Preliminary Examination

The Prelims consists of two compulsory papers:

Paper Subject Duration Marks Nature
Paper I General Studies 2 hours 200 Counted for merit
Paper II CSAT (Civil Services Aptitude Test) 2 hours 200 Qualifying (33% minimum)

Main Examination

The Mains examination consists of 9 papers, all of which are descriptive in nature:

Paper Subject Duration Marks
Paper A Indian Language (Qualifying) 3 hours 300
Paper B English (Qualifying) 3 hours 300
Paper I Essay 3 hours 250
Paper II General Studies I 3 hours 250
Paper III General Studies II 3 hours 250
Paper IV General Studies III 3 hours 250
Paper V General Studies IV 3 hours 250
Paper VI Optional Subject Paper I 3 hours 250
Paper VII Optional Subject Paper II 3 hours 250

Personality Test (Interview)

The final stage carries 275 marks, bringing the total marks for the Mains examination to 2025.

Eligibility Criteria

UPSC Eligibility Criteria Overview

Nationality

  • For IAS and IPS: Candidate must be a citizen of India
  • For other services: Citizen of India or subject of Nepal/Bhutan or Tibetan refugee who came to India before 1st January 1962

Age Limit

  • Minimum age: 21 years
  • Maximum age: 32 years (general category) as of 1st August of exam year
  • Age relaxation available for reserved categories

Educational Qualification

Candidates must hold a degree from any recognized university or possess an equivalent qualification.

Preparation Tips

Effective UPSC Preparation Strategies

Key Preparation Strategies

  • Understand the syllabus thoroughly: The UPSC syllabus is your most important document
  • Read NCERT books: Build your foundation with Class 6-12 NCERT books
  • Follow current affairs: Read newspapers like The Hindu or Indian Express daily
  • Practice answer writing: Essential for Mains examination
  • Take mock tests: Regular practice improves time management
  • Revise regularly: Revision is key to retention
  • Choose optional subject wisely: Select based on interest and availability of resources
